Internet geography, also called cybergeography, is a subdiscipline of geography
that studies the spatial organization of the Internet from social, economic,
cultural, and technological perspectives.^[1]^[2] The core assumption of
Internet geography is that the location of servers, websites, data, services,
and infrastructure is key to understand the development and the dynamics of the
Internet. Among the topics covered by this discipline are information geography
and digital divides.^[3]
